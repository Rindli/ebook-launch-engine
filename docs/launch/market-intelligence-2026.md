# Market intelligence: KDP vs private storefront

## Bottom line

For a **high-ticket niche tech e-book priced above $47 in 2026**, the more profitable primary channel is usually a **private storefront** and the better secondary discovery channel is **Amazon KDP**.

My recommendation is:

1. Launch first on a private storefront for margin, customer ownership, bonus stacking, and upsell control.
2. Use Amazon later as a lower-priced discovery layer or abridged edition rather than the flagship offer.

## Why the private storefront wins

- **Pricing freedom:** Amazon KDP's 70% royalty option applies only in certain territories and price bands. Outside those conditions, the fallback can be 35%, which is a poor fit for a premium information product.
- **Data ownership:** On a private storefront, you own the checkout flow, post-purchase journey, and customer email capture. On Amazon, the customer relationship primarily belongs to Amazon. This is an inference from the marketplace structure and the lack of native direct buyer contact surfaced in KDP's seller-facing materials.
- **Bundle economics:** A private checkout lets you package the PDF with bonuses, update packs, and the Amodei-Bot upsell in a single funnel.
- **Offer design:** Direct checkout pages are much better for high-context positioning, premium framing, testimonials, and objection handling than a marketplace detail page.

## Where KDP still helps

- **Reach:** KDP gives instant exposure to Amazon's discovery system and trust layer.
- **Friction:** Many buyers already have stored payment details and Kindle habits.
- **Credibility:** Some readers still view Amazon availability as a legitimacy signal.

## Practical channel strategy

- Sell the flagship direct at `$47`.
- Offer a future KDP edition at a lower anchor price if you want discovery.
- Keep the direct edition differentiated with bonuses, updates, and the RAG companion.

## Economics snapshot

- **Stripe direct:** Standard US card pricing is listed at `2.9% + 30c` per successful domestic card transaction.
- **KDP:** KDP states authors can earn either `35%` or `70%` royalties on digital books, subject to pricing and territory rules.
- **Inference:** Even before considering upsells and email capture, a direct store at this price point typically preserves more strategic value per buyer than a marketplace sale.
